One of the most ambitious agroecology field studies in North America — collecting comprehensive biometric inventories (soil carbon, microbiology, insect diversity, bird abundance, crop nutrient analysis, farm economics) from farms in various stages of regenerative transition. Each farm receives an individual Grower Report; the aggregate enables continental-scale analysis.

The 1000 Farms Initiative deploys field teams across North America to conduct comprehensive environmental inventories — measuring soil chemistry, microbiology, insect diversity, bird abundance, water dynamics, plant community diversity, pest presence, crop nutrients, and farm economics. The dataset bridges the gap between regenerative agriculture claims and rigorous, farm-level empirical evidence.

The 1000 Farms dataset operates on a grower-consent model. Individual farm-level data is returned to the farmer first; aggregate data is available for research and ecosystem use under specific terms.

✓ Public / Open
  • Aggregate statistical summaries by region & practice

  • Published research papers using the dataset
⚠ Consent-Gated
  • Farm-identified GPS coordinates
  • Individual farm economic data
  • Farmer identity and contact information
  • Raw per-farm biometric records

Farmer data sovereignty: Ecdysis returns full data to each participating grower before any aggregation. Farmers choose what enters the shared dataset. This consent model aligns with the Ag Knowledge Concordance "Data & Trust" framework and AKC consent-forward principles.

Dataset Context
The 1000 Farms Initiative fills a critical gap: rigorous, farm-level biometric evidence for regenerative agriculture at continental scale. Unlike university plot studies, it measures real farms under real management — and returns the data to the farmers first.

Interoperability & Integration

Compatible standards: Modus (soil lab data) · USDA WSS format · eBird (bird surveys) · FAIR data principles

Works well with: farmOS · SurveyStack · TSIP (soil carbon modeling) · Cool Farm Tool (GHG) · Point Blue bird surveys

Known gaps: No formal API yet. Machine-readable aggregate CSV on request. GPS coordinates withheld — regional analysis only. Economics data consent-gated.

Consent & Sovereignty Notes

Each farm participates voluntarily. Farmers review their Grower Report before any data enters the aggregate pool. Exact farm locations are withheld; analysis uses regional/state-level geography only.

Model: Data returns to farmers first. Economic data requires separate explicit consent and is reported only in aggregate. This aligns with the AKC "Data & Trust" framework: trust before tech, consent-forward sharing, reciprocity and accountability.
